# Shockwave Therapy Program Guide

## The Case for Cash-Pay

Most chiropractors who invest in a shockwave machine make one of two mistakes:

They try to run treatments through insurance.

They don't package treatments properly, so patients treat it like a "one-off" session.

Both approaches lead to frustration. Insurance either denies coverage or reimburses pennies on the dollar. And one-off sessions don't give patients enough exposure to get results — which leads to drop-offs and poor word-of-mouth.

That's why the most successful clinics run cash-pay programs.

Cash-pay patients are different:

- They make faster decisions because they don't need insurance approval.
- They are more committed because they've invested in their own outcome.
- They provide your clinic with predictable revenue — no waiting on reimbursements.

Bottom line: cash-pay is what makes shockwave profitable. If you rely on insurance, the machine becomes a burden. With cash-pay packages, the machine becomes a new $10,000/month revenue stream.

**Action Step:** Commit right now: All shockwave patients will go through a cash-pay structure.

## Three-Tier Package Structure

Patients need clarity and choice — but not too much choice. The magic number is three.

Here's the proven tiered offer structure:

### 1. Anchor Package (High Ticket)

12-Session Complete Recovery Package – $1,500

Includes full course of care, evaluation, reassessment, and home care plan.

Purpose: Anchors value high so the core package feels affordable.

### 2. Core Package (Most Popular)

6-Session Rapid Relief Program – $750 ($125/session)

Perfect for the majority of common chronic pain cases.

Flexible scheduling: 2–3x per week.

Purpose: 70% of patients should land here.

### 3. Entry Package (Risk-Free Trial)

First Session Trial – $99

Includes consultation ($49) + first treatment discounted ($50).

100% applied to a package if patient continues.

Purpose: Lowers resistance for first-time patients.

This structure combines psychology (anchoring, risk reversal, best-seller framing) with practicality. It lets patients self-select based on their comfort level while keeping you in control of the decision.

## Handling Price Objections

Even though patients want relief, they'll have questions like:

- "Why is this cash-pay?"
- "Why is it so expensive?"

Here's the framework you and your staff can use:

**Compare to alternatives:**

- "Surgery can cost $5,000–$10,000 with months of downtime."
- "Injections wear off, often requiring repeat visits."
- "PT can take months, with mixed results."

**Compare to lost quality of life:**

- "If plantar fasciitis keeps you from standing or walking comfortably, what's that costing you each week?"

**ROI framing for your clinic:**

- "At $750 per package, just 15 patients = $11,250/month. That's how fast this machine pays for itself."

When you frame price in terms of value and outcomes, it shifts the conversation from cost → investment.

**Action Step:** Role-play with staff. Have them practice answering "Does insurance cover this?" and "Why is it so expensive?" until they're comfortable.

## How Patients Actually Decide to Book

Every successful ad campaign follows a natural decision path.

Patients don't go from seeing an ad to booking care in a single step. They move through stages of awareness, consideration, and action.

To match this behavior, we use a Cold → Warm → Hot funnel structure.

### Cold Traffic (Awareness)

**Goal:**
Stop the scroll and introduce a new option — without selling or diagnosing.

**Who this targets:**
People who have never heard of your clinic or shockwave therapy.

**Messaging focus:**

- Lifestyle and movement challenges
- Curiosity and discovery
- Introduction to a modern, non-invasive option

**What to avoid:**

- Diagnoses or condition names
- Pain-heavy language
- Medical claims or guarantees

**Compliant example:**
"Struggling to stay active? Discover a modern, non-invasive option designed to support movement."

### Warm Traffic (Consideration)

**Goal:**
Build trust and credibility.

**Who this targets:**
People who engaged with your ads, videos, or content but didn't book.

**Messaging focus:**

- Clinic credibility
- Experience and approach
- Why people explore shockwave therapy
- Conservative, non-invasive positioning

**What to avoid:**

- Testimonials claiming outcomes
- Before/after language
- Promises of results

**Compliant example:**
"Why more active adults are exploring shockwave therapy at [Clinic Name]. Learn about our approach."

### Hot Traffic (Decision)

**Goal:**
Encourage action with clear next steps.

**Who this targets:**
People who visited your landing page, watched videos, or showed strong intent.

**Messaging focus:**

- Limited availability
- Introductory offers
- Simple, low-friction CTA

**What to avoid:**

- Medical urgency
- Fear-based language
- Outcome guarantees

**Compliant example:**
"Limited openings available for our $99 introductory shockwave session. Book before this month fills."

## Campaign Optimization and Scaling

Your first campaigns won't be perfect. That's okay — they're data-gathering.

**What to Watch:**

- CTR (Click-Through Rate): Should be 1%+.
- CPL (Cost per Lead): Aim for $30–50.
- Cost per New Patient: $99 offer should bring in patients profitably.

**How to Scale:**

- Kill underperforming ads (low CTR or high CPL).
- Duplicate winning ads with new images or hooks.
- Slowly raise daily budget (no more than 20% every 3 days).

**Action Step:** Monitor & Adjust Weekly

In Ads Manager, go to Campaigns → View Charts.

Track these key metrics:

- CTR (Click-Through Rate) → Should be 1%+
- CPL (Cost Per Lead) → Target $30–$50
- Cost Per New Patient → Your $99 trial should convert profitably

Kill ads that aren't performing:

- If CTR is under 0.75% after $50 spent → turn it off.
- If CPL is above $75 consistently → adjust copy or creative.

Scale winners gradually:

- Increase budget by no more than 20% every 3 days.
- Duplicate best ads with new images or hooks.
- Review performance every 7 days.

## Why Objection Handling Matters

Every patient has questions — sometimes they come out as objections. An objection doesn't mean "no", it usually means "I'm not sure yet."

When your team handles objections well:

- Patients feel understood, not pressured.
- Confidence goes up (in both the treatment and your clinic).
- Conversion rates on packages increase dramatically.

Framework to remember: **Acknowledge → Reframe → Proof → Invite.**

Example flow:

- **Acknowledge:** "I understand why you'd ask that."
- **Reframe:** "Here's the real story…"
- **Proof:** "Many patients like you found…"
- **Invite:** "That's why we recommend starting with the $99 trial."

**Action Step:**

- Write the framework (Acknowledge → Reframe → Proof → Invite) on a whiteboard in your staff area.
- Have each staff member practice turning one common objection into this 4-step response.
- Repeat weekly until it feels natural.
