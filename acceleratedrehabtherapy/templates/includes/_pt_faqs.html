ACCELERATED REHAB THERAPY FAQ

Q: What should I wear to my first appointment?

A: Ideally, you should wear loose clothing that will allow the therapist to assess and treat your injury. For example, if you are seeing a therapist for a shoulder injury, you may want to wear a tank top or shirt that can be easily pulled up over your head. You should also avoid wearing jewelry, as it may get in the way or be damaged.

Q: Why should I receive physical therapy for my injury?

A: Physical therapy is an important part of the healing process for many injuries. It can help speed up recovery time, reduce pain, and improve mobility. Physical therapy may also be recommended after surgery to help regain strength and range of motion.

Q: Are physical therapists doctors?

A: No, physical therapists are not doctors. However, they have extensive training in anatomy and physiology, as well as in the treatment of injuries and illnesses.

Q: Will my physical therapy be covered by insurance?

A: It depends on your insurance policy. Many policies cover at least a portion of physical therapy visits. Be sure to check with your insurance company to find out what is covered.

Q: How long will it take for me to get better?

A: This varies from person to person and depends on the nature of the injury. However, most people see significant improvement after a few weeks of physical therapy.

Q: Can I continue my regular exercises?

A: It is best to check with your therapist before continuing any exercises you were doing before your injury. In some cases, it may be safe to continue doing some exercises; in other cases, it may be necessary to modify or stop them altogether.

Q: Is physical therapy painful?

A: This varies from person to person and depends on the nature of the injury. Some people experience minor discomfort during physical therapy sessions; others do not feel any pain at all. Your therapist will work with you to make sure that the treatments are as comfortable as possible.

Q: What should I expect on my first visit?

A: Your first visit will likely include an assessment of your injury or condition and an evaluation of your current level of function. The therapist will then develop a treatment plan specifically for you and begin administering treatments. Be sure to ask any questions you have during your first visit.
