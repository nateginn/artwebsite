Common Questions

Most commercial insurance plans do not cover shockwave therapy, but that is actually good news for you. It means no prior authorization delays, no claim denials mid-treatment, and no confusing bills arriving weeks later. You get clear pricing upfront, and the $89 first visit means you are not committing to a full program until you have experienced it firsthand.
Shockwave is classified as an elective advanced therapy by most carriers, which means insurance routinely excludes it. What this means for you: no waiting on approvals, no claim fights, and total price transparency. Compared to surgery ($5,000-$10,000 with months of downtime) or repeated injections that wear off, a shockwave program is a fraction of the cost, with outcomes designed to last.
Most patients see meaningful improvement in 3-6 sessions. Complex or long-standing conditions may benefit from a full 12-session program. Your provider will evaluate your condition at your first visit and give you a realistic plan before you commit to anything beyond the initial session.
Standard PT alone often stalls out on conditions that haven't responded to rest. We pair the same movement-based rehab principles with shockwave therapy, which is specifically designed to restart healing in tissue that has stopped progressing on its own.
Most patients describe shockwave as a deep pressure or vibration, similar to a focused deep tissue massage. Sensitive areas may feel mildly uncomfortable, but it is generally well-tolerated. Your provider adjusts intensity based on your feedback throughout the session.
